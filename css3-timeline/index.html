SUGARCANE

  • Day -4

    Plough the land up to 45 cm depth

  • Day -3

    Apply 25 tons per hectare of well decomposed Farm Yard Manure (FYM) or decomposed molasses or compost
    1.Compost Tea
    2.Worm Castings
    3.Feather Meal
    Apply them 2-3 inches below

  • Day -2

    Make ridges and furrows with 80 cm spacing having 20 cm height and up to 10 m length

  • Day -1

    Apply 375 kg Super Phosphate in the furrows and wait for 1 day

  • Day 0

    1.Select about 75,000 two-budded setts per hectare from 6-8 months old nursery or raise seedlings in poly bags with single-budded setts
    2.Treat the setts for 10 minutes by dipping in a solution prepared with 125 gms of Bavistin, 2.5 kg urea and 2.5 kg lime in 250 litres of water.
    3.Plant the setts 2 cm deep with buds on the sides.For every 10 furrows, plant setts on 2 rows in one furrow

  • Day 2

    To control weeds, spray Atrataf @2.5 kg/ha in 500 litres of water with hand sprayer.

  • Day 4

    Spread sugarcane trash up to a height of 15 cm on the ridge.

  • Day 18

    Rainfall predicted in your area, no need of watering crops. In case of no rainfall, apply 1 inch water

  • Day 25

    Gap-fill with seedlings raised in polybags or the plants taken from the 2 rows planted- furrows in every 10th furrow.

  • Day 30

    1.Mix 5 kg Azospirillum and 5 kg phophobacterium per ha mixed with 250 kg powdered FYM.
    2.Apply at the bottom of the plants and irrigate immediately.

  • Day 35

    1.From 35 to 100 days after planting Irrigate once in 7-10 days To prevent attack by early shoot borer, apply chlorpyrifos @ 250-300 g a.i./ ha on the setts and cover with soil.
    2.If 25-30% of the shoots are affected, then for every 100 metre length of furrow, mix chlorpyrifos @ 250-300 g a.i./ in water and apply using a hand sprayer on the tips and bottom of the shoots.

  • Day 41

    Irrigate (Sprinkler irrigation is the suitable method to satisfy the requirement, during initial stages.) 2-3 cm depth

  • Day 42

    1.Does any Sugarcane affected by grassy shoot?
    2.What is the color of grass now?

  • Day 45

    1.Do hand weeding
    2.Apply in pits a mixture of 110 kg Nitrogen, 60 kg of Potash and 35 kg of Neem cake per hectare.

  • Day 48

    Irrigate 2-3 cm depth

  • Day 55

    Irrigate 2-3 cm depth

  • Day 60

    1.Spray a mixture of Urea 2.5%, Potassium chloride 2.5% during periods of drought.
    2.Apply a mixture of 5kg azospirillum, 5kg phophobacterium and 250 kg of decomposed FYM in powder form at the bottom of the plants and irrigate immediately.

  • Day 62

    Irrigate 2-3 cm depth

  • Day 69

    Irrigate 2-3 cm depth

  • Day 76

    Irrigate 2-3 cm depth

  • Day 90

    do hand weeding; apply in pits (after earthing up) a mixture of 110kg of Nitrogen, 60 kg of Potassium and 35 kg of neem cake per hectare.

  • Day 113

    1.which symptoms did you notice till now? red rotGrassy ShootLeaf Spotsett rot
    2.What is the color of grass now?

    3.What is the color of internode now?

    4.What is the height of sugarcane now?
    5.Is there any Drought condition?

  • Day 120

    Under drought conditions, apply 60 kg of potassium and irrigate immediately

  • Day 150

    Carry out de-trashing

  • Day 150 to
    Day 225

    If inter-node borer exists, release parasites 6 times @5 cc per hectare once in 15 days 101-210 days - irrigate once in 7 days 210th day- Again de-trash and tie lodged canes 225th day- Spary monocrotophos 36 SL @ 600 g a.i./ha to control mealy bugs, white fly and scales

  • Day 260

    Spray Dichlorvas 76%EC (300 g a.i./ha) to control pyrilla and all sucking pests, if necessary.

  • Day 270 to
    Day 360

    Irrigate once in 15 days.
    Stop irrigation 15 days before harvesting

  • harvest

    Cut canes at the bottom close to the ground with sickles or sharp knife. Remove trash, roots, water shoots and cane tops and send clean canes to the factory.
